AI Roadmap
To become a AI Professional you need to know
01
Fundamentals

a. Mathematics:
Linear Algebra
Calculus
Probability and Statistics

b. Programming:
Python (or another programming language like R or Julia)
Data Structures and Algorithms

c. Machine Learning:
Basic concepts: Supervised, Unsupervised, Reinforcement Learning
Model evaluation and validation
Feature engineering

02
Intermediate

a. Deep Learning:
Neural Networks
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
Transfer Learning
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)
Attention Mechanisms

b. Natural Language Processing (NLP):
Tokenization, Stemming, Lemmatization
Named Entity Recognition (NER)
Word Embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe)
Sequence-to-Sequence models
Transformer models (e.g., BERT, GPT)

c. Computer Vision:
Image Preprocessing
Object Detection
Image Segmentation
Image Classification

03
Advanced:

a. Reinforcement Learning:
Markov Decision Processes
Q-Learning
Policy Gradient Methods
Deep Reinforcement Learning

b. Model Deployment and Productionization:
Model Optimization
Containerization (e.g., Docker)
Deployment Platforms (e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)

c. Ethical and Responsible AI:
Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency in AI (FAT/ML)
Bias and Fairness Evaluation
AI Regulation and Ethics

04
Specializations and Applications:

a. Robotics:
Robot Kinematics and Dynamics
Control Theory
Robot Perception
b. Autonomous Vehicles:
Sensor Fusion
Path Planning
Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Navigation

c. Healthcare:
Medical Image Analysis
Clinical Decision Support Systems
Drug Discovery

d. Finance:
Algorithmic Trading
Fraud Detection
Risk Management

e. Natural Language Generation (NLG):
Text Summarization Dialogue Systems Content Generation

05
Continuous Learning:

Stay updated with the latest research papers, conferences, and online courses.

Engage with the AI community through forums, social media, and meetups.

Work on personal projects and contribute to open-source AI projects.

Known for its groundbreaking work in deep reinforcement learning and artificial general intelligence (AGI). DeepMind's AlphaGo defeated world champion Go player Lee Sedol, marking a milestone in AI's ability to master complex games.

OpenAI

Recognized for its advancements in natural language processing (NLP) and large-scale AI models. OpenAI developed GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) models, including GPT-3, which demonstrated impressive capabilities in text generation and understanding.

NVIDIA

Pioneered in hardware acceleration for AI with its GPUs. NVIDIA's GPUs have become a standard choice for training deep learning models due to their parallel processing capabilities and performance.

IBM Watson

Known for its cognitive computing platform, Watson, which has been applied across various industries, including healthcare, finance, and customer service. IBM Watson's capabilities include natural language understanding, machine learning, and data analytics.

Microsoft

Notable for its Azure AI platform, which provides a range of AI services and tools for developers and businesses. Microsoft has made significant contributions to AI research and development, particularly in computer vision and speech recognition.

Amazon Web Services (AWS)

Leading provider of cloud-based AI services, including machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision. AWS offers scalable and cost-effective solutions for AI development and deployment.

Facebook AI Research (FAIR)

Conducts cutting-edge research in AI and machine learning, contributing to advancements in computer vision, language understanding, and reinforcement learning. FAIR has developed state-of-the-art models and frameworks, such as PyTorch.

Tesla

Revolutionizing the automotive industry with its AI-driven autonomous driving technology. Tesla's Autopilot and Full Self-Driving capabilities leverage deep learning and computer vision algorithms to enable advanced driver assistance features.
